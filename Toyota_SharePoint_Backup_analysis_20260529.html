How RUs work & why SLA length matters

RUs are Microsoft's internal cost metric for API calls. Throttling is enforced against the RU budget, not raw request count.

API classCost
Permission-related APIs5 RU per call
All other APIs1 RU per call

Every backup pays a fixed cost in metadata APIs regardless of how much changed. Backing a site up daily pays this metadata tax 3 times in 3 days; backing it up once every 3 days pays it once — making a 3-day SLA materially cheaper in RU terms.

How steady-state demand is derived

Today only ~70k of ~190k sites are actively backing up. Scaling linearly:

20 M × (190k / 70k) ≈ ~54 M RUs per 3-day window

The 12 M RU/day figure reflects sites backed up every day. Backing them up once every 3 days means more changes accumulate per backup — so each backup costs more (~20 M over the 3-day window). Different cadences, not the same workload measured twice.

Calculations & assumptions

Inputs: start 75k of ~190k sites; 1 App = 105 M RU/week; ~665 RU/week to keep a site compliant; ~9,800 RU to onboard one site (600 TB spread uniformly over the 120k pending sites, at ~0.5 TB per 1 M RU). Static backlog assumed — no further accrual on waiting sites (optimistic).

Each week, capacity first maintains the compliant set; leftover headroom onboards new sites:

N(w+1) = N(w) + (105M − 665×N(w)) / 9,800 = 0.9321×N(w) + 10,714

This converges to a ceiling set by capacity ÷ maintenance, independent of backlog size:

Ceiling = 105M / 665 ≈ 157,900 sites ≈ 83%

Backlog size only sets how fast the ceiling is reached (half-life ~10 weeks), not its height. Weekly gain crosses below 1.5% (~2,850 sites) at Week 11.

Questions & Answers

Backlog & Capacity Backlog

What is the basis for the ~600 TB pending backlog figure?
~950 TB pending across ~450 large drives. ~60% is backup-eligible, minus data already ingested → ~500 TB. Plus ~100 TB of accumulated incremental change for January-only sites = ~600 TB.
What is the basis for the ~100 TB incremental estimate?
~100k sites last backed up in January 2026. Per-site change rate extrapolated over ~100 days:
100,000 × 5.5 × 4 MB × 100 days × 0.5 ≈ ~110 TB
Last successful backups span June 2025–Jan 2026. Using the midpoint gives ~200 TB. Is this correct?
Before the new scheduling strategy began in late January, incremental backups were scheduled randomly, so most sites do have a January backup. We verified the timings from the database, confirming the ~100-day basis.
How does RU consumption change over time as backlog and incrementals coexist?
Today ~70k sites use ~20 M RUs / 3 days. Steady state (all 190k) = ~54 M. Capacity (2 Apps) stays at 90 M. Headroom drops from ~70 M to ~36 M, averaging ~50 M per cycle for backlog clearance.
How is the ~36 M headroom figure derived?
90 M − 54 M = ~36 M RUs of headroom in steady state

SLA Choices SLA

Why is a 1-day SLA infeasible while a 3-day SLA works?
Each backup includes fixed metadata APIs every time, plus content APIs proportional to changes. One backup every 3 days eliminates two of three metadata rounds per site, saving a large fraction of the RU budget.
Given ~100 TB/month growth, what is the expected timeline including that growth?
The ~100 TB/month growth is already factored into the steady-state 54 M RU / 3-day demand figure. It does not need to be counted again.
Has the 7-day SLA proposal been dropped?
Yes. Beyond ~3 days the delta link expires, making 4+ day SLAs impractical.
Does "complete resynchronization required" mean differential extraction becomes unreliable after 4 days?
Yes. On day 4 the delta link is invalid, so all items must be re-fetched — consuming substantially more API calls.
The April 23 figure was 27.40%, not ~44%. Why the discrepancy?
Compliance fluctuates daily. Over the past week, 27.4% was the low and 44% the high — best expressed as a range (27.4% – 44%).

Resource Units Methodology RU

What is the basis for the 7.89 TB/day processing figure?
600 TB / 76 days ≈ 7.89 TB/day
What is the daily processable capacity for the 1-App configuration?
With a single App, steady state is not sustainable at this scale — the backlog may never clear.
Is there official Microsoft documentation supporting the 15 M RU/day figure?
Microsoft's public docs state 6 M RU for tenants with >50k licenses, but we observe ~15 M in practice on this tenant. Effective limits vary by tenant size.
Could the remaining 120k sites have different usage patterns than the active 70k?
The 70k are a random subset, so usage patterns are assumed broadly similar. We will refine the estimate as more sites enter steady state.
What constraint determined the 20 M RU / 3-day number — throttling or Rubrik concurrency?
The 20 M figure is an estimate of demand, not an observed throttling limit. It is derived from the distribution of metadata vs content APIs. We can share the per-API breakdown if needed.
Why is the 3-day estimate (~20 M) lower than the daily observed (~12 M)?
12 M/day reflects daily backups. Backing up once every 3 days means more changes accumulate per backup — ~20 M over 3 days. Different cadences, not the same workload measured twice.
Is the remaining ~3 M RUs (20% not used by daily incrementals) consumed by initial backups?
Yes — that residual budget is used by the heavy-drive initial backups (Block A).

App Expansion Ops

Will the 2-App proposal affect other Enterprise Apps using SharePoint Online?
The second app registration gets its own RU quota — it does not change throttling for unrelated apps. We can work with BIPROGY to scope the operational and security review before rollout.
Does this mean even with two apps, the 1-day SLA cannot be met?
Correct. Even with 2 Apps the daily demand exceeds the combined daily quota at full scale; 3-day SLA is the practical ceiling.
Can we start with 1 App on a 3-day SLA, observe, then decide on expansion?
A staged approach is reasonable. Moving to a 3-day SLA under 1 App lets both sides observe real RU utilization and backlog trend. We recommend agreeing in advance on the metrics and thresholds that would trigger expansion, so the decision is not delayed.
